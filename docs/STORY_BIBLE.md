# Story bible

This is the canonical high-level reference. Expand details only after they are accepted into the game.

## Premise

Eighty years after an empire's collapse, three successor kingdoms preserve an uneasy peace through official history, political bargains, and selective violence. A failed border war threatens that peace when evidence suggests powerful people on both sides deliberately created it.

## Central question

If revealing the truth would begin another war, is the truth still worth protecting?

## Creative principles

- No chosen hero or purely evil faction
- Motives matter more than moral labels
- Violence has social, physical, and political costs
- Magic, if introduced, remains rare, feared, and difficult to understand
- Cynical humor comes from character perspective, never from dismissing consequences
- Inspiration may inform tone and construction, but characters, worldbuilding, plot, and prose must remain original

## Viewpoint characters

### Garren Vale

Former military interrogator returning from a failed war. He reads weakness well and treats conversation as conflict. He claims people never change while secretly needing evidence that he can.

### Lady Elianor Vey

Politically ignored second daughter of a powerful house. She wants to protect her younger brother and resents manipulation while becoming skilled at it herself.

### Oren “the Hollow”

Condemned criminal serving an unofficial royal agent. Humor protects whatever he genuinely values. His stated desire for freedom hides why he allowed himself to be captured.

## Opening situation

Garren reaches a rain-soaked military checkpoint carrying a sealed letter taken from a dying commander. A deserter named Tavin claims the regiment's destruction at Red Hollow was planned. The first decisions establish how Garren handles danger, information, loyalty, and opportunity.
