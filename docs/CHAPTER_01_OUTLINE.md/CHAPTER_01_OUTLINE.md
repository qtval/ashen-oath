# Chapter One Outline: The Carrion Road

## Purpose

This document is the approved planning layer for the first one-hour story slice. It comes before expanding `data/chapters/chapter_01.json` so that the branching structure, character motives, and delayed consequences remain coherent.

The chapter should feel like a grim medieval thriller told through close, distinctive viewpoints: dry humor under pressure, people protecting self-serving stories about themselves, and choices where every advantage creates a different cost. These are high-level narrative qualities, not an imitation of any living author's prose.

## Chapter promise

Garren Vale returns from the failed border war carrying a sealed letter taken from Captain Orl, his dying commander. At a military checkpoint outside his home town, Garren discovers that the Crown is searching for a deserter who claims the regiment's destruction at Red Hollow was deliberate.

By the end of the chapter, Garren must decide what kind of danger he prefers:

- the danger of being useful to the authorities;
- the danger of protecting a witness;
- the danger of selling information to survive; or
- the danger of keeping the truth without knowing what it will cost others.

The player should understand that the letter matters, but not yet know the complete conspiracy. Chapter One opens a wound; it does not solve the war.

## Player experience targets

- Approximately 45–60 minutes on a first playthrough.
- 5 acts with a clear escalation in risk.
- 30–40 story scenes/nodes after implementation.
- 3–5 endings or end states, with meaningful variation in Garren's relationships and information.
- At least 3 delayed consequences that are established early and paid off late.
- No choice labelled as morally correct.
- Most mechanical effects remain hidden; the player reads intent and consequence through character behavior.

## Tone and writing rules

- Keep the world original: no borrowed names, plots, factions, terminology, or signature lines from existing fiction.
- Use black humor as a defense mechanism or social weapon, never as a way to make suffering meaningless.
- Let characters be competent in different ways and foolish in different ways.
- Give every important conversation a stated position, an unstated need, and a pressure point.
- Prefer concrete details—wet wool, bad teeth, ration marks, a clerk's ink-stained thumb—to exposition dumps.
- Violence is quick, ugly, and politically consequential. It is never a clean solution.
- Garren's internal voice should be observant and defensive, not omniscient. He can misread motives.

## Core cast for this chapter

### Garren Vale — returning interrogator

- Wants: reach home and learn why Captain Orl sacrificed the regiment.
- Claims: he only wants to be left alone.
- Fears: that he is useful only when hurting or frightening people.
- Self-deception: he calls suspicion wisdom because trust once cost him soldiers.
- Breaking point: someone uses a dead soldier's name as a bargaining chip.
- Chapter movement: from controlling information to choosing who deserves it.

### Tavin Rusk — deserter and witness

- Wants: survive long enough to expose the order behind Red Hollow.
- Claims: he deserted because the army was already lost.
- Hidden truth: he stole a copy of the order and left another soldier holding the blame.
- Voice: fast, defensive, and practical; jokes only when panic is winning.
- Relationship variables: trust, fear, and whether Garren learns his real name.

### Captain Meret Rusk — checkpoint commander

- Wants: restore order and earn a promotion before the border scandal reaches the capital.
- Claims: the checkpoint is protecting civilians.
- Hidden truth: she received a sealed instruction to identify anyone carrying Captain Orl's evidence.
- Voice: calm, bureaucratic, and politely threatening.
- Pressure point: her younger brother is among the missing at Red Hollow.

### Sella Venn — merchant under pressure

- Wants: get her wagon and surviving family through the gate before night.
- Claims: she has no interest in soldiers or politics.
- Hidden truth: she has been moving ration ledgers for a faction that is not paying her.
- Voice: blunt, economical, and unimpressed by military authority.
- Relationship variables: debt, respect, and whether Garren risks her cargo.

### Brother Caldus — ruined-chapel keeper

- Wants: keep the chapel neutral and its refugees alive.
- Claims: he is only a tired priest.
- Hidden truth: he once copied military seals for the people now hunting Tavin.
- Voice: patient, bleakly funny, and precise about hypocrisy.
- Function: offers Garren a non-military interpretation of the evidence.

### Captain Orl — dead commander, present through evidence

- Wants (revealed through documents): preserve proof that the battle was arranged.
- Contradiction: he ordered Garren to interrogate prisoners while secretly trying to save his own name.
- Function: complicates Garren's desire for a clean victim and a clean villain.

## Chapter structure

### Act I — The Gate in the Rain

**Goal:** establish Garren's return, the checkpoint, the letter, and the first collision with Tavin.

Key beats:

1. Arrival on the Carrion Road; refugees and wagons are held outside the gate.
2. Garren observes the checkpoint and notices a search pattern aimed at one wagon.
3. Tavin recognizes him beneath Sella's wagon and names Red Hollow.
4. The player chooses to expose, hide, bargain with, or ignore Tavin.
5. Captain Meret notices Garren's military bearing and begins a formal search.

Act I choices establish: caution, observation, ruthlessness, opportunism, Tavin's trust, Sella's debt, and Meret's suspicion.

### Act II — A Search for Useful Men

**Goal:** force Garren to choose how visible he becomes and reveal that the checkpoint search is targeted.

Key beats:

1. The checkpoint is locked down after a false alarm or a discovered body.
2. Meret questions Garren about Captain Orl and the surviving regiment.
3. Sella demands a decision about whether Tavin remains hidden in her wagon.
4. Garren finds a ration ledger or search order bearing a familiar seal.
5. Tavin reveals that the order sent the regiment into a position already marked for death.
6. Garren must choose one temporary alliance: Meret, Sella, Tavin, or no one.

Act II consequence: one ally becomes available in Act III, while another begins to distrust Garren.

### Act III — The Chapel Without Bells

**Goal:** move the conflict outside the checkpoint and widen the mystery without explaining everything.

Key beats:

1. Garren escapes, negotiates passage, or is escorted to the ruined chapel.
2. Brother Caldus shelters refugees while soldiers search the marsh.
3. Tavin admits he did not merely run: he left a wounded comrade with the order copy.
4. Garren can inspect three clues: a seal impression, a ration ledger, or a survivor's testimony.
5. A messenger arrives with an offer from an unnamed faction: exchange the letter for safe passage and a new identity.
6. The player decides whether to show the letter to Caldus, conceal it, or use it as bait.

Act III consequence: the evidence becomes more credible but Garren's preferred explanation becomes less comfortable. Captain Orl may have participated before trying to expose the plan.

### Act IV — What the Dead Are Worth

**Goal:** make every alliance demand a price and bring the checkpoint conflict into the chapel.

Key beats:

1. Meret's soldiers surround the chapel, or a rival faction arrives first.
2. Sella's wagon is stopped with civilians still inside; her survival depends on Garren's earlier promise.
3. Tavin proposes killing a prisoner or forging a seal to create an escape route.
4. Caldus reveals his old connection to the military seals.
5. Garren learns the letter contains two layers: a public order and a hidden annotation in Orl's hand.
6. The player chooses a plan: negotiate, deceive, flee through the crypt, sacrifice an ally's reputation, or use violence.

Act IV consequence: one character's breaking point is reached. The chapter's late state is determined by who Garren protected, humiliated, or treated as expendable.

### Act V — The Road Decides

**Goal:** resolve the immediate chase while leaving the larger conspiracy alive.

Key beats:

1. The chapel burns, falls under Crown control, or becomes a temporary refuge depending on Act IV.
2. Garren must decide what happens to the letter: deliver, conceal, destroy, or split its evidence between people.
3. Tavin chooses whether to stay with Garren, flee alone, or trade Garren's location for protection.
4. Meret records an official version of the checkpoint incident.
5. Garren reaches the road toward home—or turns toward the border with evidence and enemies.

The ending should resolve the checkpoint crisis but create a new obligation for Chapter Two.

## Branch map

The chapter uses four narrative axes rather than a good/evil meter:

| Axis | Low expression | High expression | Delayed use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Caution | acts before certainty | waits and gathers leverage | ambush and search checks |
| Ruthlessness | avoids needless harm | treats people as tools | ally breaking points |
| Opportunism | keeps promises at cost | trades favors and evidence | faction offers |
| Observation | misses or dismisses clues | notices patterns and contradictions | hidden annotation and Act V reveal |

Relationship state should remain separate from these tendencies:

- `tavin_trust` and `tavin_fear`
- `sella_debt` and `sella_respect`
- `meret_suspicion` and `meret_favor`
- `caldus_trust`
- `letter_status`
- `evidence_strength`
- `official_story`

## Planned ending families

These are outcome families, not final titles yet:

1. **The Crown's Witness:** Garren is officially useful; the letter is controlled by the authorities.
2. **The Marsh Alliance:** Tavin survives and Garren leaves with an imperfect but independent witness.
3. **The Burned Truth:** the evidence is destroyed or scattered to protect living people.
4. **The Purchased Road:** Garren trades information for safety, money, or a new identity.
5. **The Empty Victory:** Garren exposes one conspirator but loses the person or evidence that mattered most.

Each family should vary by ally survival, public reputation, and what Garren believes about Captain Orl.
